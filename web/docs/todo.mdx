## Written units

<WrittenUnitTable locale={locale} />

**Written form <TOD links="Wb" />**. Written form <TOD links="Wb" /> (used in Todo letter <TOD links="w" />) is similar to the written form <TOD links="D" /> (used in Hudum letter <TOD links="d" />), but since the form <TOD links="Wb" /> comes from the bent <TOD links="W" /> and thus from _beth_, and the form <TOD links="D" /> from _lamedh_, the two are represented differently.

## Phonetic letters

The variants in gray indicate the fabricated variants, _i.e._ variants borrowed from other joining positions. The variants in yellow indicate the variants that are unattested in the modern orthography of Traditional Mongolian.

<LetterTable locale={locale} />

**Final <TOD links="I3" /> in letter <TOD links="y" />, medial <TOD links="O" /> and final <TOD links="U" /> in letter <TOD links="w" />**. Final <TOD links="I3" /> in letter <TOD links="y" />, medial <TOD links="O" /> and final <TOD links="U" /> in letter <TOD links="w" /> are introduced in [GB/T 36649—2018](https://openstd.samr.gov.cn/bzgk/gb/newGbInfo?hcno=289D5B6AB1E6B17173C5F5842D9B0C8D), where the written form <TOD links="I3" /> of letter <TOD links="y" /> and the written form <TOD links="O" /> of letter <TOD links="w" /> in the examples <TOD links="t u w l a y" />, and the written form <TOD links="U" /> of letter <TOD links="w" /> in the example <TOD links="d a r u w" /> are all differences derived from letter analysis. Since <TOD links="t u u l a i" /> and <TOD links="d a r u u" /> are preferred, the written forms are marked in gray.

**Initial <TOD links="A I" /> and medial <TOD links="I" /> in letter <TOD links="i" />, medial <TOD links="O" /> and final <TOD links="O" /> in letter <TOD links="oe" />, written form <TOD links="Y" /> in letter <TOD links="y" />**. These written forms are introduced in [GB/T 36649—2018](https://openstd.samr.gov.cn/bzgk/gb/newGbInfo?hcno=289D5B6AB1E6B17173C5F5842D9B0C8D), showing the cognition between Todo <TOD links="e" /> and Hudum <MNG links="ee" />, Todo <TOD links="i" /> and Hudum <MNG links="i" />, Todo <TOD links="oe" /> and Hudum <MNG links="oe" />, Todo <TOD links="y" /> and Hudum <MNG links="y" />. Since cognition is not considered a basis for establishing a written form system, the letters in the running text are not analyzed for these forms with cognition associations, and therefore these written forms are marked in gray.

**Initial <TOD links="N" /> in letter <TOD links="n" />**. A written unit is used for particle <TOD links="– n i" /> in Todo, and is regarded as a stylistic variant of the written unit <TOD links="N" />, thus is not triggered by FVS.

**Letter <TOD links="ny" />**. The letter <TOD links="ny" /> is introduced in [蒙汉词典 (增订本)](https://search.worldcat.org/zh-cn/title/867334994), but is not found in Todo literature. The appearance is subsequently succeeded by [GB/T 36649—2018](https://openstd.samr.gov.cn/bzgk/gb/newGbInfo?hcno=289D5B6AB1E6B17173C5F5842D9B0C8D). The character is used by Hudum Ali Gali and Todo Ali Gali.

### Conditional mapping

<ConditionalMappingTable locale={locale} />

**Function of the sign <TOD links="ƚ" />**. The sign <TOD links="ƚ" /> behaves as a base character in some cases, but its logic in the shaping process behaves as a combining mark. For this reason, the necessary preprocessing and postprocessing should be introduced: before the Mongolian-specific steps begin, <TOD links="ƚ" /> is shaped in mark class, modifying the joining type of the characters before and after <TOD links="ƚ" />; after the Mongolian-specific steps finish, those signs <TOD links="ƚ" /> that need to be represented as marks are shaped to ligature with the neighboring letters. These extra steps are not included in the condition table shown above, but are completed in the specific implementation.
