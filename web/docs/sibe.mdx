**Final <Mong locale="SIB" links="A I" /> in letter <Mong locale="SIB" links="i" />**. Final <Mong locale="SIB" links="A I" /> in letter <Mong locale="SIB" links="i" /> is introduced in [GB/T 36641—2018](https://openstd.samr.gov.cn/bzgk/gb/newGbInfo?hcno=D1B6A81C6BF961CE4265E816103F0020), where the written form <Mong locale="SIB" links="A I" /> of letter <Mong locale="SIB" links="i" />, in the examples <Mong locale="SIB" links="j u i" />, is the differing form derived from letter analysis. Since <Mong locale="SIB" links="j u sbm i" /> is preferred, the written form is marked in gray.

**Letter <Mong locale="SIB" links="j" />**. Since the establishment of the Sibe–Solon Cultural Association in 1947, the Association has made modifications to the Sibe orthography. In the scheme given by the Association, both the initial and medial forms of the letter <Mong locale="SIB" links="j" /> use the written form <Mong locale="SIB" links="I" />. However, in the subsequent practice of using the Sibe writing system, it was found that this modification would cause confusion of words. Therefore, in all kinds of recently published books, newspapers and dictionaries, the written form <Mong locale="SIB" links="J2" /> is used instead of <Mong locale="SIB" links="I" />.

**Letter <Mong locale="SIB" links="z" />**. Unlike Manchu, in the Sibe <Mong locale="SIB" links="z i" />, the stroke in <Mong locale="SIB" links="z" /> evolved into a long tooth, forming a <Mong locale="SIB" links="z i" /> ligature. In the literature, the <Mong locale="SIB" links="i" /> part of the Sibe <Mong locale="SIB" links="z i" /> ligature is considered equivalent to the <Mong locale="SIB" links="i" /> part of the ligature with bowed letter, which led [GB/T 36641—2018](https://openstd.samr.gov.cn/bzgk/gb/newGbInfo?hcno=D1B6A81C6BF961CE4265E816103F0020) to introduce the upper part of the <Mong locale="SIB" links="z i" /> ligature that is considered to belong to <Mong locale="SIB" links="z" />. However, this part never appears separately in non-ligature places and is therefore not considered as separate written forms of the letter <Mong locale="SIB" links="z" />.
