## Phonetic letters

<LetterTable locale={locale} />

**Final <SIB links="A I" /> in letter <SIB links="i" />**. Final <SIB links="A I" /> in letter <SIB links="i" /> is introduced in [GB/T 36641—2018](https://openstd.samr.gov.cn/bzgk/gb/newGbInfo?hcno=D1B6A81C6BF961CE4265E816103F0020), where the written form <SIB links="A I" /> of letter <SIB links="i" />, in the examples <SIB links="j u i" />, is the differing form derived from letter analysis. Since <SIB links="j u sbm i" /> is preferred, the written form is marked in gray.

**Letter <SIB links="j" />**. Since the establishment of the Sibe–Solon Cultural Association in 1947, the Association has made modifications to the Sibe orthography. In the scheme given by the Association, both the initial and medial forms of the letter <SIB links="j" /> use the written form <SIB links="I" />. However, in the subsequent practice of using the Sibe writing system, it was found that this modification would cause confusion of words. Therefore, in all kinds of recently published books, newspapers and dictionaries, the written form <SIB links="J2" /> is used instead of <SIB links="I" />.

**Letter <SIB links="z" />**. Unlike Manchu, in the Sibe <SIB links="z i" />, the stroke in <SIB links="z" /> evolved into a long tooth, forming a <SIB links="z i" /> ligature. In the literature, the <SIB links="i" /> part of the Sibe <SIB links="z i" /> ligature is considered equivalent to the <SIB links="i" /> part of the ligature with bowed letter, which led [GB/T 36641—2018](https://openstd.samr.gov.cn/bzgk/gb/newGbInfo?hcno=D1B6A81C6BF961CE4265E816103F0020) to introduce the upper part of the <SIB links="z i" /> ligature that is considered to belong to <SIB links="z" />. However, this part never appears separately in non-ligature places and is therefore not considered as separate written forms of the letter <SIB links="z" />.
